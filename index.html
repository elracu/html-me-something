Yosemite National Park

I visited Yosemite back in 2010, and I have to say it's one of the most beatiful places I've been. I remember drving into the valley at night and sensing this huge white wall on either side of the road. It was the walls of the rock mountains that surround it. The moonlight made them look like pale imposing giants. We stopped the car in the middle of the road and look up in awe at a sight that I'll never forget. And guess what? That was just the beginning

Here's a brief description of the park from the National Park Service Website:

Not just a great valley, but a shrine to human foresight, the strength of granite, the power of glaciers, the persistence of life, and the tranquility of the High Sierra. First protected in 1864, Yosemite National Park is best known for its waterfalls, but within its nearly 1,200 square miles, you can find deep valleys, grand meadows, ancient giant sequoias, a vast wilderness area, and much more.

I would highly recommend visiting this park if you like hiking up mountain trails. For more information click here

Fort McHenry National Monument and Historic Shrine

Fort McHenry is actually a National Monument and not a park. This page does a pretty good job of explaining the distiction between one and the other from the administrative perspective of the National Park Service. I visited Fort McHenry back in 2011 and I really enjoyed learning about it's historical relevance in the War of 1812 and how the "The Star-Spangled Banner" was written during one of the battles of the war. There's reenactments with actors dressed in period attire and all kinds of historical artifacts. To lear more about this monument click here
